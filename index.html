02 — Research & Speaking Mumbai University × Fortis Hospital

Machines that read mammograms

Best Application Paper — IIT Roorkee-hosted conference

Enhancing Interpretability and Diagnostic Accuracy in CNN-Based Mammography Analysis

A dual-task CNN — a customized Xception — that classifies breast density across four classes and malignancy in one pass, trained on 7,632 clinically annotated mammograms in collaboration with Fortis Hospital. Iterated from a 75% baseline to 99.7% published density-classification accuracy.

The paper focuses on diagnostic accuracy and explainability — a model radiologists can interrogate, not just trust. In press: Springer Proceedings in Mathematics & Statistics, 2026.

Off the page: invited speaker at Xavier Institute of Engineering's AI in Healthcare outreach program — a session on Programming NLP in Healthcare, exploring AI and natural language processing in clinical contexts (Sept 2025).
